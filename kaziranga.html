The One-Horned Legend

Nourished by the Brahmaputra, Kaziranga is home to the world’s largest population of Great Indian One-Horned Rhinoceroses.Kaziranga National Park, located in the floodplains of the Brahmaputra River in Assam, covers approximately 430 square kilometers and represents one of the last unmodified natural areas in northeastern India UNESCO UNESCO +1 . It was established as a reserve forest in 1905, declared a national park in 1974, and inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1985 for its outstanding universal value under natural criteria.Kaziranga is globally recognized for harboring the largest population of the Indian one-horned rhinoceros, currently estimated at over 2,600 individuals Wikipedia Wikipedia +1 . The park also provides habitat for other threatened species, including tigers, Asian elephants, wild water buffalo, swamp deer, gaur, Sambar deer, hog deer, capped langur, hoolock gibbon, and sloth bear UNESCO UNESCO +1 . Aquatic species such as the Ganges River dolphin and over 450 species of birds, including migratory species like pelicans and storks, further highlight its ecological importance

Visions of the Wild

UNESCO Status

Declared a World Heritage Site in 1985 for its unique natural environment.

It was first established as a Reserved Forest in 1905, thanks to the efforts of Mary Curzon.

Best Time to Visit

Nov to April: The park is open and the weather is pleasant.

Note: The park remains closed from May to October due to monsoon floods.

  • Elephant Safari: Approx ₹900 per person

  • Location: Kaziranga National Park, Assam, India
