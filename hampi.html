Ruins of The Vijayanagar Empire

Hampi City

H

The Lost City Of Kings

Hampi, the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its magnificent ruins. The city was once a thriving center of trade and culture, but now stands as a testament to its glorious past. The ruins of Hampi are spread across a vast area, featuring grand temples, palaces, and market streets. The Virupaksha Temple, with its towering gopuram, is one of the most iconic structures in Hampi. The Vittala Temple complex is renowned for its musical pillars and the stone chariot, which is a symbol of Hampi's architectural brilliance. The city's strategic location along the Tungabhadra River added to its prosperity, making it a hub for traders from across Asia. Today, Hampi attracts visitors with its surreal landscape dotted with boulders and ancient structures, offering a glimpse into the grandeur of the Vijayanagara Empire. Hampi, once the flourishing capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, is today a vast landscape of ancient ruins set amid striking rocky terrain. Known for its grand temples, markets, and intricate stone structures, the city reflects the architectural brilliance and cultural richness of a powerful medieval kingdom. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hampi stands as a reminder of a glorious past, where every monument tells a story of art, trade, and royal legacy.

Remnants of Glorious Past

UNESCO Status

Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986, Hampi is recognized for its outstanding group of monuments that reflect the grandeur of the Vijayanagara Empire. The site is celebrated for its unique blend of religious architecture, royal complexes, marketplaces, and intricate stone carvings spread across a vast landscape.

Best Time to Visit

Sunrise & Sunset: The soft golden light enhances the dramatic boulder landscape and ancient ruins.

October to February: The most pleasant season, with cooler temperatures ideal for exploring the expansive site.

Entry Details

  • Entry Fee: ₹40 (Indians) / ₹600 (Foreigners) (for major monuments; some areas are free)
  • Hours: 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM (timings may vary by monument)
  • Location: Hampi, Vijayanagara District, Karnataka, India
